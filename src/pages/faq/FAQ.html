What is the student council?

The student council is a group of students elected by their peers to represent the interests and voice of the entire student body. The council works closely with school administrators to improve student life, plan events and activities, and address any concerns or issues students may have.

Who can vote in the student council elections?

All currently enrolled students at the University of San Carlos are eligible to vote for student council representatives in their respective grade levels and for the student body president.

How do I vote?

Voting takes place entirely online through this secure voting platform. You'll need to log in with your student email and password. Clear instructions will guide you through the voting process once logged in.

How can I get involved with student council?

Students not elected can still participate by joining council committees or attending meetings. Check for announcements or contact your grade-level representatives.
